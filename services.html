French Tourism

French tourism spans mountains, lakes, parks, and of course Paris.

France has many tourist destinations to offer and is the most visited country on Earth. Paris claims the title of top visited city in the world with over 30 million visitors per year, but France has a lot to offer outside of Paris. French countryside and mountains are great locations for skiing and quiet getaways. Disneyland is also the top tourist attraction in Europe with over 15 million visitors per year.

Musee du Louvre, arguably the most famous art museum in the world, sits on the right bank of the River Seine. Home to the Mona Lisa among other famous works, The Louvre sees millions of visitors per year. Extending from the Louvre Pyramid, Jardin de Tuleries is a lovely spot to walk around or relax on a sunny day. There are many fountains with chairs around them to sit and read or just people-watch. (Metro Stop.....Louvre-Rivoli, Palais Royal/Musee du Louvre, Tuleries)

Centre Pompidou is an architectural spectacle due to its interesting design with the pipes on the outside. It is known as an art and cultural center, but its top attraction is the National Museum of Modern Art. It displays a massive collection of contemporary art pieces and is a must see. It is situated in a great area within walking distance from many other tourist destinations such as Notre Dame and The Louvre. (Metro Stop.....Chatalet, Hotel de Ville)

Parc des Buttes-Chaumont is arguably the most beautiful park in Paris. It is situated in and around a large lake basin with massive grass hill going up around it. The hills are great for a picnic or to enjoy a bottle of wine. There is a large gazebo that sits at the highest point of the park, and this is the best place to watch the sunset in Paris. (Metro Stop.....Buttes Chaumont, Laumiere)

There are very few better ways to see the sights of Paris than from the River Seine. There are lots of options for Seine boat cruises for all budgets. These cruises take visitors past the Tower, Notre Dame, Musee d'Orsay among other sights. (Metro Stop.....Alma Marceau)

Normandy is one of the premier spots in the world for historical tourism. It is home to the towns of Caen, Bayeux, and others. There are many museum options. The Bayeux Tapestry is centuries old and tells a tale of Normandic military conquest. The Caen-Normandie Memorial Museum is a spectacular display of WW2 history.

The Beaches and Cemetaries are must see. The American Cemetary is situated just inland from Omaha Beach and the cemetary incredibly sobering reminders of the D-Day losses. Omaha Beach is a regular beach where visitors can swim, paddleboard, and relax.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most breathtaking destinations on Earth. The massive bronze tower stands high above the Parisian skyline, and has a light show at night for the first 5 minutes of every hour.

Arc de Triomphe

The Arc de Triomphe stands at the Northwest end of the Champs Elysses. It is a great spot to get a spectacular view of the city of lights. The Champs Elysses is the premier shopping street in the world.

Sacre-Coeur

The Sacre-Coeur rests on Montmarte, the highest natural point in Paris. It is a massive Roman Catholic basilica made of travertine stone, giving it the white color. Montemarte is a another great place to look out over the city.

French Sports Tourism

France is a leader in world soccer. In 2018, the France Mens National Soccer Team won the World Cup. They also hosted the 2019 Womens World Cup. France also is the home country of a few world renowned clubs such as PSG and Marseille.

Each June, Stade Roland Garros hosts the French Open tennis tournament. It is one of four "major tournaments" with players such as Federer, Nadal, and others playing. It is played on red clay courts and is a notalble attraction for tennis fans across the world.
